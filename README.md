# Insider Threat & IAM Risk Analysis

## Project Overview
This project involves a forensic investigation into an internal data integrity breach and unauthorized privilege escalation. I analyzed a security incident where administrative credentials were used to bypass internal controls, resulting in the unauthorized modification of sensitive academic and financial records.

## Key Skills Demonstrated
* **Insider Threat Detection:** Analyzing behavioral anomalies and credential abuse by internal actors.
* **IAM Governance:** Evaluating the failure of **Separation of Duties** and the **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)**.
* **Forensic Accountability:** Utilizing **Non-Repudiation** and system logs to attribute unauthorized actions to specific users.
* **Risk Remediation:** Developing administrative and technical deterrent controls to prevent future data integrity breaches.

## Investigation Methodology
The audit was structured around three primary security pillars:
1. **Access Control Review:** Distinguishing between "Right to Access" and "Authorization of Intent."
2. **Integrity & Attribution:** Correlating system telemetry with user credentials to disprove claims of account compromise.
3. **Remediation Strategy:** Proposing IAM hardening measures to mitigate the "Insider Threat" attack vector.

## Environment & Certifications Applied
* **Framework:** NIST SP 800-53 (Access Control & Audit Accountability)
* **Certifications:** CompTIA Security+, ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)
